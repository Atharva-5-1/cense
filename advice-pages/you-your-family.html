Cyber Security Advice for You & Your Family

Securing Your Digital Life

In today's connected world, protecting your family's digital life is as important as locking your front door. From online banking and shopping to social media and schoolwork, our lives are increasingly online. This guide provides practical steps you and your family can take to stay safe from common cyber threats.

1. Master Your Passwords

Strong passwords are your first line of defense. A strong password is long, complex, and unique for each account.

  • Use a Password Manager: These tools create, store, and fill in strong, unique passwords for all your accounts.
  • Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): 2FA adds a second layer of security, requiring a code from your phone in addition to your password. Enable it on all important accounts like email and banking.

2. Recognize and Avoid Phishing Scams

Phishing is when criminals send fake emails or text messages to trick you into giving them your personal information. Teach your family to look for these red flags:

  • Urgent or threatening language.
  • Requests for personal information or passwords.
  • Suspicious links or attachments.
  • Spelling and grammar mistakes.

Rule of thumb: If an email or message seems suspicious, it probably is. Don't click any links. Instead, go directly to the official website or app.

3. Secure Your Home Wi-Fi Network

Your home router is the gateway to all your connected devices. It's crucial to secure it properly.

  • Change the default administrator password on your router.
  • Use WPA3 or WPA2 encryption for your network.
  • Create a separate "guest" network for visitors to use.
